Shubhavi Arya was born on 7 October, 1998 in New Delhi, India. She is a junior double majoring in computer science and psychology at University of Minnesota, Twin Cities.

Shubhavi Arya has a deep passion for integrating technology with psychological science and this passion has led her to pursue interdisciplinary research in various psychology and computer science labs where she has worked using various programming languages including, Python, JavaScript, React Native, Docker, MongoDb and testing using Jest.

She is a Research Intern at the Lim Lab in Department of Psychiatry, University of Minnesota where she is working on, NIH EXAMINER, a neuropsychological test battery to assess domains of executive function. Shubhavi Arya has been supervised by Dr. Kelvin Lim, MD at the Lim Lab. EXAMINER was originaly designed in Psychopy 1.76 and Shubhavi Arya has re-developed it in Psychopy 3 to suit the growing demands of psychological science.

Shubhavi Arya is also a ProDUCT Researcher at the GroupLens Lab, Department of Computer Science working under Professor Lana Yarosh. She is part of a team working on a web based app FlipDoubt developed in JavaScript, MongoDb, CSS, HTML and testing using Jest. The application is an attempt at supporting and enriching the mental health issues of the society. The application uses Amazon MTurk to present a positive version and perspective of negative thoughts entered by the user in the application. A different perspective on user's negative thoughts and frustrations can lead to decreased anxiety, happiness, lower stress and increased motivation. Shubhavi Arya is actively engaged in the GroupLensens lab pursuing research in the intersection between computer science, social computing and human-computer interaction.

Shubhavi Arya is a Research Assistant at the Health Psychology Lab, University of Minnesota and is supervised by Erin Standen and Dr. Alex Rothman. Shubhavi's experience in this lab has been very multidisciplinary. She has been responsible for programming the calorie tracking mobile app 'Hearty Carrot's sdk and making necessary changes in the app for progressing the research study. She has used React Native, JavaScript and Expo to support the development of the mobile app 'Hearty Carrot'. The research study at this lab is one of the first studies investigating the utility and impact of calorie-tracking apps and to identify which features are maximally helpful and minimally harmful to people's behavioral and psychological health.

Shubhavi Arya wears multiple hats and is also a decorated animator and rising film director. She is most noted for creating cutout stop-motion animated film Adventures of Malia at a young age of 16 years. Adventures of Malia was nominated for 11 international film awards, winning 3 of them including the Honorary Mention Foreign Film Award at the ColorTape International Film Festival, Australia and Best Under 18 Filmmaker at the Global Independent Film Awards, United States. Shubhavi Arya has written, directed and edited 7 films including a feature length film, short films, documentary films and animated films. Her films have been selected in over 10 countries in over 40 international film festivals and have won over 5 international awards. She has recently completed a short film 'Aniyah' and has been working for over a year on color-grading and post-production process. She is proficient in DaVinci Resolve, Avid Media Composer, Adobe Premiere Pro, Tangent panels, Flame, Maya, Toon Boom Harmony and Stop Motion pro.

At school, Shubhavi Arya is very involved around campus. She is part of the International Latin Dance Club at UMN, Ballroom Dance Club, Internet of Things Collaborative and Alpha Theta Tau.

She was a merit-based scholarship holder in grade 9 and 10 and did her two years of schooling in Singapore. In grade 10, Shubhavi took 7 CIE IGCSE exams as a private candidate while also attending regular IB school. She was later home-schooled during grade 11 and 12 and pursued UK A-Level exams as well as AP exams and excelled in both. She was also the recipient of the Academic Scholarship for grades 11 and 12 worth S$10,000 at the German European School, Singapore in 2014. She was shortlisted for the merit-based IB Diploma Scholarship worth INR 4,600,000 at Global Indian International School, Singapore. In 2009, she also received academic scholarship worth INR 800 by the International Children Equity Fund for excelling at national level in Math, Science and English in India.

She has actively participated in many math and science competitions. She participated in the American Mathematics Competition, Singapore Junior Chemistry Olympiad and the Indian National State Physics Olympiad (NSEP). She also took the Cayley Math Contest and the Galois Math Contest by the University of Waterloo, Canada.
